🏆 Exercise Guide
👁️
Eye Area & Orbital Bone
Squint exercises: close eyes tightly for 3s, relax. 3×10 reps. Look side to side with only your eyes (not head) slowly. Helps reduce puffiness and define the orbital area.
🦷
Jaw & Masseter
Chew mastic gum daily (20–30 min). Wide jaw stretch: open mouth as wide as possible, hold 5s. Side-to-side jaw slides: 3×15. Builds the masseter for a stronger jawline.
👄
Lip & Philtrum
Pursed lip hold: pucker lips, hold for 10s, release. 3×10. Keep lips together and relaxed throughout the day to shorten philtrum over time.
💆
Cheekbones & Zygomatic
Big smile hold: smile as wide as possible showing teeth, hold 10s. 3×10. Combine with mewing to push tongue against palate — together these help widen the midface.
🧘
Neck & Chin
Chin tucks: retract chin straight back (like making a double chin), hold 5s, repeat 15×. Neck curls: lying down, lift head slightly off pillow, 3×12. Reduces neck fat and improves forward head posture.

😴 Sleep Guidance

Sleep is when your body releases growth hormone and repairs tissue. This is arguably the most important looksmaxxing variable — no stack or routine compensates for poor sleep.

⏰
Target 8–9 Hours
Growth hormone peaks during the first few hours of deep sleep. Getting 8–9 hours consistently supports bone density, skin cell turnover, collagen production, and fat metabolism — all directly impacting looks.
🌙
Fixed Sleep Schedule
Go to bed and wake at the same time every day — including weekends. Your body's circadian rhythm regulates cortisol and testosterone. A regular schedule lowers baseline cortisol, which directly reduces puffiness and acne.
📱
No Screens 1 Hour Before Bed
Blue light suppresses melatonin. Dim lights, avoid phone/TV for 60 min before sleep. Use orange or red tinted lights in the evening. This alone dramatically improves sleep quality and next-day skin appearance.
🛏️
Sleep Position
Sleep on your back where possible. Side sleeping causes sleep wrinkles and facial asymmetry over time from years of pressure. Back sleeping also reduces puffiness as fluid drains away from the face. Use a firm pillow to maintain neck alignment.
🌡️
Cool, Dark Room
Optimal sleep temperature is 16–19°C. Darkness triggers melatonin — use blackout curtains or an eye mask. Cold air + darkness = deeper sleep = more GH release.
💧 Water Intake Guidance

Hydration directly affects skin plumpness, under-eye puffiness, acne frequency, and overall facial appearance. Dehydration makes skin look sunken, dull, and aged.

💧
Daily Target: 3–4 Litres
Aim for 3L minimum on rest days, 4L+ on training days. The standard "8 glasses" is a minimum — looksmaxxers aim higher. Spread intake throughout the day rather than drinking large amounts at once.
🌅
First Glass on Waking
Drink 500 mL of water immediately on waking — before coffee, before food. This flushes cortisol, rehydrates after sleep, and kickstarts lymphatic drainage which reduces under-eye puffiness.
🧂
Salt Your Food — Don't Fear It
On this diet, salt generously — sodium is needed for muscle function and aldosterone regulation. The raw milk and beef naturally support sodium-potassium balance. Avoid processed sodium (ready meals, sauces) but use real sea salt freely on food.
☕
Limit Caffeine After 2 pm
Caffeine is a diuretic and disrupts sleep if consumed late. Stick to 1–2 coffees before noon. Replace afternoon drinks with water, raw milk, or herbal tea. Better sleep = better skin = better looks.
🧍 Posture Guidance

Good posture dramatically changes how you look and carry yourself — it makes you appear taller, more confident, and your facial structure becomes more visible when your neck is upright.

🦷
Mewing Posture — Tongue on Palate
Keep the full tongue resting on the roof of your mouth at all times. This positions the lower jaw forward, lifts the hyoid bone, and when done consistently over years, influences forward facial growth and jaw definition.
📐
Forward Head Posture Fix
Do chin tucks 3×15 daily. When sitting, ears should be directly above shoulders — not in front. Every centimetre of forward head posture adds ~5kg of strain on the cervical spine and rounds the upper back, which makes you look shorter and weaker.
🏋️
Upper Back & Neck Strength
Train the posterior chain: face pulls, band pull-aparts, rear delt flyes, and rows. Weak rear delts and traps cause rounded shoulders — this makes your frame look narrower and less imposing than it is. Aim for 2–3 posture-specific sets daily.
🪑
Desk & Phone Habits
Hold your phone at eye level — not chin-level. At a desk, screen should be at eye height, chair adjusted so hips are above knees. Set a posture reminder every 30 min. Prolonged neck flexion (looking down at phone) accelerates neck ageing and affects jawline over time.
😮‍💨
Nasal Breathing — Always
Mouth breathing widens the lower face, drops the chin, and narrows the palate over time. Keep lips sealed and breathe through your nose at all times — during exercise, sleep, and at rest. Tape your mouth shut at night if you mouth-breathe during sleep (use medical tape).

🧂 Na:K Food Catalogue

Foods at or better than a 1:2 sodium-to-potassium ratio — ideal for debloating and sodium balance

Why this ratio matters: The WHO recommends eating at least twice as much potassium as sodium (1:2 Na:K by weight). Potassium actively flushes sodium out of your cells, reduces water retention, lowers blood pressure, and counteracts bloating. Most processed foods have the ratio backwards — this catalogue shows you what to eat to fix that. A ratio of 10:1 K:Na or higher is exceptional. 3:1–9:1 is good. 2:1 is the minimum target.

☁️ Cloud Sync Setup
Cloud sync uses Firebase Realtime Database (free, by Google) so your wellness data stays in sync across all your devices in real time. Setup takes about 5 minutes.
Step 1 — Create your Firebase project
  1. Go to console.firebase.google.com and sign in with Google
  2. Click Add project → name it anything (e.g. "My Wellness") → Continue
  3. Disable Google Analytics if asked (not needed) → Create project
  4. In the left sidebar, click Build → Realtime Database
  5. Click Create Database → choose any location → Start in test mode → Enable
  6. Go back to Project Overview → click the </> Web icon to add a web app
  7. Give it a nickname → Register app
  8. Copy the firebaseConfig object shown and paste it below ↓
Step 2 — Set a secret key (optional but recommended)
This is a private passphrase that hides your data in the database. Use the same key on all your devices. Can be anything — e.g. josh2025wellness
